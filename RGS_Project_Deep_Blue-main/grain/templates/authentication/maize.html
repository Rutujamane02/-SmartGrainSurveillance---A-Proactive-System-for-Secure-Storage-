Maize Production and Export in India

Source: FAO (Updated as on 01-12-2023) & UN-COMTRADE data 2022

India is the 5th largest producer and 14th largest exporter of maize in the world. It is highly valued globally for its multifarious use as food, feed, fodder, and as a raw material for a large number of industrial products.

India's Advantage

With wide adaptability, maize can be grown in regions ranging from sea level to up to 3000 meters above mean sea level. India has a strategic and geographical advantage for maize exports, including:

  • Round-the-year maize production
  • Well-established seed production and marketing network
  • Availability of sea-ports

However, domestic demand remains high, making significant maize export less feasible at the moment.

Maize Uses

Maize is used as a staple food, animal feed, and as a raw material in many industries such as:

  • Starch
  • Oil
  • Protein
  • Alcoholic beverages
  • Pharmaceuticals
  • Cosmetics
  • Film
  • Textiles
  • Packaging and paper industries

Areas of Cultivation

Major maize-growing states in India include Karnataka, Madhya Pradesh, Bihar, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Maharashtra, and Andhra Pradesh.

India's Maize Production

The production of maize in India for 2023-24 is estimated at 35.67 million tonnes (3rd Advance Estimate).

Maize Exports

India exported 14,42,671.48 metric tonnes of maize, valued at Rs. 3660.10 crores (USD 443.53 million) in 2023-24. Major export destinations include Vietnam, Nepal, Bangladesh, Malaysia, and Thailand.
